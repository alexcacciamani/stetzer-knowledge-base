# Must-Know Insights from the Spec Home Roundtable Sessions

The Builder's Edge program featured several dedicated spec builder roundtables and related discussions. Here are the essential takeaways, synthesized across all relevant sessions.

---

## 1. Financing Structures & Finding Capital

### The 50/50 Investor Partnership Model
The most frequently discussed financing structure involves a capital partner funding the deal while the builder contributes expertise and earns compensation through multiple streams.

> "I use partners on all my new builds. So I come in with no money out of pocket. Partner gets 50% of the profit. I get 50% of profit. I do charge a GC fee of 10% ish of the build price and I do get a real estate commission on the backend."
— **2025-11-11 — Spec Builders Roundtable: Financing, Margins, and Building Strategy**

### Investor Return Thresholds
There's a clear floor for attracting private capital:

> "25 is the number. Below that it's tough to find money. Below 20, like, cause there's less risky investments."
— **2025-11-11 — Spec Builders Roundtable**

### Use Land as Collateral Before Giving Up Equity
If you already own lots, don't default to equity partners:

> "If you own the lots, why have you thought about just using them as the collateral for a bank loan? Because you'll save a lot of money. If you're talking about giving up 50% of your equity versus paying between seven and a half and eight and a half percent, those are drastically different returns."
— **2025-11-11 — Spec Builders Roundtable**

### Build Multiple Banking Relationships
No single bank will always lend:

> "There's three banks that I'm using right now because periodically the banks stop lending on new construction for spec. So you have to build relationships with several banks."
— **2025-11-11 — Spec Builders Roundtable**

### Finding Investors Through Soft Pitching
The best approach is low-pressure and relationship-driven:

> "I want to make it really easy for them to say no because if you want to say no I don't want you. I don't want your money. I don't want you to ask me about everything."
— **2025-11-11 — Spec Builders Roundtable**

Investors are often emotionally motivated: *"A lot of the people that invest in me are kind of living vicariously through me. They wish they were builders or developers."*

## 2. Profit Margin Targets

### Target 30%, Accept 20%
> "You want to make 30% profit. You might end up with 20, but the number to shoot for is 30."
— **2025-12-09 — Spec Home Selling Strategies, Lot Selection, and Market Timing**

### 20% Return on Total Project Costs as a Developer Minimum
This accounts for all capital deployed — interest, selling fees, holding costs — not just construction costs.
— **2025-11-11 — Spec Builders Roundtable**

## 3. Selling Strategy: Timing, Pricing & Presentation

### Sell Finished, Staged, and Pre-Inspected
This was one of the strongest consensus points across all roundtables:

> "You get one shot to hit the market. You want to hit the market at the right time of the year for your specific market, but finished, punch list done, staged, pre-inspected, ready to go."
— **2025-11-11 — Spec Builders Roundtable**

### Seasonal Selling Windows Matter Enormously
> "We have like a three month window where it's 10% higher than the rest of the year. And if we miss that window, it's like, on a million, $2 million house, $3 million house, that's a lot of money."
— **2025-12-09 — Spec Home Selling Strategies**

### Never List During Construction
Listing during the build accumulates days on market, which signals problems to buyers. One participant's home sat over 200 days because of this mistake.

> "You could have a projected end date for your listing while it's under construction. And then you take it off the market for whatever your MLS requires you to, to reset the clock."
— **2025-12-09 — Spec Home Selling Strategies**

### The First Five Photos Rule
> "Change your photos. The description doesn't matter. Buyers will look through to look at the first five photos. First five photos would get them sucked in. Just think Instagram."
— **2025-12-09 — Spec Home Selling Strategies**

### The "Long Skirt" Listing Strategy
Don't over-share in the listing:

> "I think if you give too many pictures and too much information, sometimes I think that the people will rule out the house before going to see it. So it's kind of like long skirt instead of mini skirt. Just enough where they want to go see the house."
— **2025-12-09 — Spec Home Selling Strategies**

### Price Reduction Rules of Thumb
> "The rule of thumb: showings but no offers is 5%. No showings is at least 10."
— **2025-12-09 — Spec Home Selling Strategies**

## 4. Pre-Selling: Proceed with Extreme Caution

This was a recurring warning across multiple sessions. Pre-selling introduces massive complexity:

> "It took 4 million percent more effort on my part to get it to the finish line than a spec house. Every decision has to run by the husband, the wife, the kid, the aunt, the uncle, their friend is an architect."
— **2025-11-11 — Spec Builders Roundtable**

The roundtable from **2026-03-10** reinforced this:

> "Building on spec is nine billion times easier than building anything for a customer. It's not even remotely close."

**If you do pre-sell**, take a substantial non-refundable deposit (~20%), limit changes strictly to finishes, and convert all selections to allowances so overruns are paid by the buyer.

## 5. Lot Selection & Underwriting

### The 20-25% Land Cost Rule
> "20% is kind of the rule of thumb, 20, 25, kind of the rule of thumb that you want to pay for dirt on the ARV. So like, $5 million house, you'd want to be spending around $1.2 to $1.4."
— **2025-12-09 — Spec Home Selling Strategies**

### A-Plus Everything in Soft Markets
> "If you've got like two A's and a negative or a B or a C, then those are going to sit."
— **2025-12-09 — Spec Home Selling Strategies**

### Visit the Lot at Multiple Times
Drive or walk the lot at sunrise, sunset, and during a heavy rainstorm to understand traffic, neighbor activity, drainage, and flooding before committing.
— **2025-12-09 — Spec Home Selling Strategies**

### Market Health Metric: Percentage Under Contract
> "The tipping point seems to be about 25 to 30%... right now I'm seeing numbers like 5, 10, 15, 20."
— **2025-12-09 — Spec Home Selling Strategies**

## 6. Price Point Strategy

### Avoid the Middle
> "I need to be in the 1.5 million plus because down at the 600,000 I'm competing with all of these other companies that build a track home and just slap them up super quick and I don't want to be competing with that anyway."
— **2025-11-11 — Spec Builders Roundtable**

Interest-rate sensitivity is highest in the $600K–$1M range, where buyers are more likely to be affected by monthly payment fluctuations. Above $1.5M, buyers tend to be less rate-sensitive.

## 7. Finishes & Material Strategy

From **2026-01-13 — Spec Build Finishes, Budget Strategy, and Deal Financing**:

### Touch-and-Feel Spending Principle
> "If someone thinks that you're skimping on something that's like between $20 and $100, then like what else are you skimping on?"

Spend on items buyers physically interact with: door hardware, plumbing fixtures, flooring.

### Recognizable Brand Anchoring
> "People recognize appliance brands. So I'll spend the money for Wolf Sub-Zero on the higher-end stuff. They see that and they think everything else is more expensive."

### Line-Item Budgets for Designers
> "I give him a budget for each line item that I'm looking to him to make decisions on. I think that really helps keep everything tight rather than having a global budget."

### Don't Over-Design for Your Market
> "I've gone too cutting edge on the design. My designer lives in L.A. and everybody in Boulder wanted stuff that was in style like five years ago."

## 8. Estimating & Cost Management

From **2026-03-10 — Spec Builders Roundtable: Plans, Estimating, Tools, and Selling Strategy**:

### Build a Cost Database Over Time
> "Each time you build a house you'll build a database and then when you build your next house and you estimate the next house you adjust your previous thing and you try and use as many formulas as you can."

### Percentage-Based Estimating Is Unreliable for Custom Specs
> "If you're building a custom house every time, then what you spent on tile last time is not going to necessarily be relevant this time."

### Per-Square-Foot Trade Benchmarks
> "Once you get enough bids you'll start to see a pattern... my electrician wants 20 grand. If the next house is three thousand square feet and he wants 30 grand, now you know basically he's billing you out at ten dollars per square foot."

### Reuse Plans When Possible
> "My first is to pull from what I already have because I know what the costs are pretty much."

## 9. Holding Costs & Taking Losses Strategically

### Holding Costs Will Kill You
This phrase appeared repeatedly. Extended time on market destroys profitability through accumulated interest and carrying costs.

### Take the Loss and Redeploy Capital
> "The people that just sold it took the loss, they turned and turned and they turned their money like 10 times. So you're better off taking the small loss and just move on."
— **2025-12-09 — Spec Home Selling Strategies**

## 10. The Spec Home as a Strategic Business Tool

Even when a spec doesn't sell quickly, it can serve multiple purposes:

> "It has acted as a model home and I've gotten a handful of builds in the last six months just from walking people through it."
— **2026-01-13 — Spec Build Finishes**

From **2025-04-09 — Open Workshop**, Erin also noted that building a spec home creates tangible proof of quality when transitioning from remodeling to new construction or moving into higher-end markets. And from the **2025-09-22** session, the advice was to use completed spec homes strategically by hosting community events like art shows to build relationships and attract custom home clients.

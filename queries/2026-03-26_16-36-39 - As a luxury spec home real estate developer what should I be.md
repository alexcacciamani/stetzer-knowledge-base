# Daily, Weekly, and Monthly Operating Cadence for a Luxury Spec Home Developer

Based on a comprehensive synthesis of Erin Stetzer's Builder's Edge coaching program, here is a structured operating cadence drawing from teachings across multiple sessions.

---

## DAILY Non-Negotiables

### Mindset & Energy
- **Start positive intentionally.** Erin teaches that humans default to negativity, so you must deliberately set a positive, energized mindset each morning before anything else hits you.
  > "When I get up — this is so funny — I'm like, today's going to be awesome. Like today's gonna be a great day." *(2025-12-18 — Brand Identity and Seasons of Business)*

- **Review your annual goals at least once** (Erin recommends three times — morning, afternoon, and before bed). Keep them on your phone for quick access.
  > "You get what you focus on, not what you want." *(2025-07-10 — Erin's Daily Fundamentals System)*

- **Exercise and morning routine are non-negotiable infrastructure** — not optional. This fuels the energy required to serve others all day.
  > "I've been at the gym at one thirty in the morning to make it happen. It doesn't matter. It is not skipped." *(2026-01-29 — Annual Planning Framework)*

### Job Site & Project Management
- **Visit every active project every single day**, even if only for five minutes. This is Erin's "every house, every day" rule.
  > "Every house, every day, period. That is what happens. It's not negotiable." *(2025-10-30 — Project Manager Playbook)*

- **Enter from a different point each visit** to force fresh observation. Habitual entry routes cause your brain to stop registering defects.
  > "If you enter in the front door every single time as a project manager, you're not going to see things because it just becomes part of ho-hum." *(2025-10-30 — Project Manager Playbook)*

- **Record at least three videos per project per day.** Video forces you to slow down and observe details that photos miss. These also serve as documentation and progress records.
  > "When they're taking these videos, they're literally slowing their brains down." *(2025-10-30 — Project Manager Playbook)*

- **Look at ceiling, walls, and floor in every room** and find at least 10 things to question or correct before leaving site. *(2025-11-06 — Open Workshop: Project Management)*

### Financial Vigilance
- **Review your financial position daily** — bank accounts, outstanding draws, incoming invoices. Erin faced this every day when building her company and teaches it as foundational.
  > "When I first started the company, I was in the bank account every day. I was on paying the bills. I knew exactly where everything was." *(2025-09-04 — Cash Flow Projection)*

- **Process invoices daily.** Incoming invoices go to a holding file; only approved invoices move to the pay file. Never let this accumulate.
  > "Not once a week, not every other week. It's every single day." *(2025-10-23 — Company Structure and Financial Systems)*

### Relationships & Business Development
- **Create three opportunities per day** through conversations — calls to architects, designers, agents, trades, or potential investors. Do this *regardless* of how you feel.
  > "I do X regardless. Regardless was the word he used. And it stops there." *(2025-07-10 — Erin's Daily Fundamentals System)*

- **Post on social media daily.** At minimum, three Instagram stories per day plus one main feed post. This is how prospects feel they know you before ever meeting you.
  > "The rule is post three stories a day, no matter what." *(2025-06-10 — Brand Identity and Social Media Consistency)*
  > "All of moving a prospect to a client happens when nobody's watching." *(2026-02-26 — How To Move A Prospect To A Client)*

### Documentation & Wins
- **Log five wins at the end of each day** — personal and professional. This builds self-belief and provides evidence of progress during hard seasons.
  > "We take for granted, and oftentimes we may not recognize, how we did add value to someone's life." *(2025-07-10 — Erin's Daily Fundamentals System)*

- **Capture two insights from ordinary observations** to train pattern recognition. *(2025-07-10 — Erin's Daily Fundamentals System)*

## WEEKLY Cadence

### Project Operations
- **Own your calendar for the week.** Block fixed, recurring time slots for each project site visit and communicate that schedule to all trades so they bring questions to those times.
  > "My entire calendar was every minute filled out to where I was going to be for that entire week. Repeat, repeat, repeat." *(2025-10-23 — Company Structure and Financial Systems)*

- **Send weekly project reports** on the same day every week, without exception. Include a four-week rolling look-ahead schedule with a "hot list" of urgent items.
  > "My only goal in weekly reports is to not get a phone call or an email because I didn't explain something well." *(2025-05-08 — Open Workshop)*
  > "The four weeks is enough to give people closure on the details." *(2025-05-08 — Open Workshop)*

- **Follow up on all outstanding subcontractor bids and trade commitments.** Use the structured Trade Tracker with color-coded accountability — confirm plans sent (Day 1), receipt confirmed (Day 3), questions addressed (Day 5), loop closed (Day 10). Phone calls, not emails.
  > "The language of construction is not email… it's a phone call in person, track you down." *(2025-10-02 — Subcontractor Pricing Guide & Trade Tracker Walkthrough)*

- **Cross-reference budget vs. actual** on every active project. Ensure percent complete (financial) tracks with percent complete (construction).
  > "Where projects get in big trouble is when the percent done on the construction site is 20 but the money we're asking from a client is at 70 percent." *(2025-05-15 — Modifications Control the Process)*

### Team & Trade Management
- **Hold a weekly team meeting** (if you have staff or PMs). Include casual rapport-building, bottleneck identification, resource sharing, and breakout sessions.
  > "We're so busy running in different lanes all week long that we really don't get to kind of laugh a little bit with each other." *(2026-01-15 — Pinpointing Urgency and Weekly Team Meetings)*

- **Fill out scorecards** for all active trades, designers, and architects. Review them every Sunday night as part of weekly planning.
  > "If it's a two on that line item for four weeks, that requires a conversation with the team." *(2025-08-21 — Scorecards)*

- **Friday office hours** — set a structured end-of-week time for project managers (or yourself) to process open issues and prepare for the following Monday.

### Market & Sales
- **Review your feeder list.** Are you nurturing relationships with your top five architects, designers, agents, and vendor partners? Make at least one proactive outreach per week.
  > "I make a list… every year I have this list. It will take me sometimes four years to get into the right architect." *(2025-04-09 — Open Workshop: Feeders and Team Structure)*

- **Monitor market health.** Check percentage of homes under contract in your target zip codes and price bands. A healthy market sits above 25-30%; anything below signals caution.
  > "The tipping point seems to be about 25 to 30%." *(2025-12-09 — Spec Home Selling Strategies)*

- **Review your pipeline spreadsheet.** Map all current and upcoming projects month-by-month with projected revenue to identify future cash flow gaps before they arrive.
  > "It really helped me understand where the drop-offs were." *(2025-11-20 — Guest Expert Kelli Little on Construction Financial Systems)*

## MONTHLY Cadence

### Financial Deep Dive
- **Review full cash flow projection** — 3, 6, 9, and 12 months out. This should separate business units (new construction vs. remodeling vs. spec) because margins differ significantly.
  > "You can make decisions on your next moves when you can capture predictability in three months, six months, nine months, 12 months." *(2025-09-04 — Cash Flow Projection)*

- **Track overhead as a percentage of cost of work.** Target approximately 5% per project as a benchmark.
  > "Typically we'll see that overhead, let's just call it 5%." *(2025-09-11 — Open Workshop: Overhead and Builder Fees)*

- **Calculate your current financial position snapshot:** Outstanding client draws + current bank balance − all outstanding bills = your real position.
  > "If your business were to shut down tomorrow and you collect whatever draws are outstanding and you add that to your bank account and you cut all of your checks — how much cash do you have left?" *(2025-11-20 — Kelli Little on Financial Systems)*

- **Review holding costs on any unsold spec homes.** Carrying costs will kill profitability faster than a price reduction will.
  > "Holding costs will kill you." *(2026-01-13 — Spec Build Finishes and Budget Strategy)*

### Strategic Review
- **Evaluate every active and potential project against your goals.** Use the "people, time, and money" framework to determine fit.
  > "When it comes to clarifying our ideal client, it's so much more than just the person hiring you. It's the time, it's the people, it's the money." *(2025-07-24 — Clarifying Your Ideal Client)*

- **Analyze what season of business you're in** — winter (preparation/hardship), spring (opportunity from preparation), summer (execution), or fall (analysis). Adjust your actions accordingly.
  > "It's important to recognize what these are and then what to do with them." *(2025-06-26 — Seasons of Business)*

- **Assess your two-year positioning.** Every decision you make today positions your company one to two years from now.
  > "Every decision and choice we make positions us a year or two from now 100% of the time." *(2025-12-04 — Open Workshop)*

- **Review investor relationships and deal structures.** If using private capital, ensure annualized returns are tracking to the 25%+ threshold that keeps investors engaged.
  > "25 is the number. Below that it's tough to find money." *(2025-11-11 — Spec Builders Roundtable)*

### Listing & Sales Strategy
- **Assess listing presentation on any active specs.** Are the first five photos the strongest features? Is the listing fresh?
  > "First five photos would get them sucked in. Just think Instagram." *(2025-12-09 — Spec Home Selling Strategies)*

- **Evaluate seasonal selling windows.** Plan project completion timelines backward from peak buyer activity periods in your market.
  > "We have like a three month window where it's 10% higher than the rest of the year. And if we miss that window… on a million, $2 million house, that's a lot of money." *(2025-12-09 — Spec Home Selling Strategies)*

- **Assess stalled listings aggressively.** No showings = at least 10% overpriced. Showings but no offers = 5% overpriced. Take the loss and redeploy capital rather than bleeding holding costs.
  > "The people that just sold it took the loss… they turned their money like 10 times. You're better off taking the small loss and just move on." *(2025-12-09 — Spec Home Selling Strategies)*

### Personal Development
- **Conduct a personal and business debrief.** Review the month through the lens of your three life buckets: Energy, Work/Service, and Love/Family.
  > "When one thing overrides the other… that's where people can get lost." *(2026-01-29 — Annual Planning Framework)*

- **Update your "what needs to be true" list** for each annual goal.
  > "What needs to be true for this to happen? I'm not attached to the outcome." *(2026-01-29 — Annual Planning Framework)*

## QUARTERLY / ANNUALLY

### Quarterly
- **Conduct post-mortems on completed projects** — go cost code by cost code to identify where estimates missed. Update your cost database.
  > "Each time you build a house you'll build a database." *(2026-03-10 — Spec Builders Roundtable)*

- **Hold one-on-one meetings with each team member** focused on their personal and professional goals and alignment with the company.
  > "I have every one of them listed here and I'm constantly trying to find how we can support… what's their secret sauce?" *(2026-01-29 — Annual Planning Framework)*

- **Reassess your ideal client profile** using scorecards accumulated across projects.
  > "If you do this, you speed yourself up five years." *(2025-07-24 — Clarifying Your Ideal Client)*

### Annually
- **Run the 30-year to 1-year planning exercise.** Set goals across all life buckets, define what needs to be true, identify daily standards, and set rewards.
  > "A lot of times we go for massive goals and we hit them, but sometimes it takes longer." *(2026-01-29 — Annual Planning Framework)*

- **Update your feeder list** — top five architects, designers, agents, and trades you want to work with. Some relationships take four years to land. *(2025-04-09 — Open Workshop)*

- **Audit your brand identity.** Does your website, social media, and marketing reflect where you are going, not where you came from?
  > "How you show up is it, it's as simple as that." *(2025-12-18 — Brand Identity and Seasons of Business)*

- **Take intentional quiet time** during the holiday period to reflect, journal, and set strategic direction for the year ahead.
  > "This coaching program is a result of me taking two weeks during Christmas and writing and writing and writing." *(2025-12-18 — Brand Identity and Seasons of Business)*

## Summary: The Throughline

Across all of these sessions, Erin's overarching message for spec developers is that **operational excellence IS your marketing strategy.** The daily disciplines — site visits, financial tracking, trade communication, social media — compound over years into a reputation that attracts better investors, better agents, better trades, and better buyers.

> "95% of the industry doesn't do any of this. So, literally, just by checking the boxes here, you're now in the top 5%, maybe top 3% of how you can serve clients." *(2025-09-18 — Bridge to Bigger Builds)*

> "Operations and systems generate referrals and repeat business more effectively than traditional networking or marketing." *(2025-05-08 — Open Workshop)*

The builders who win in luxury spec are not the most talented — they are the most persistent, the most organized, and the most disciplined in doing the unglamorous daily work that nobody else is willing to do.

> "The people who do really well in life are not super bright. They're not brilliant. They're persistent and they're clear." *(2025-07-31 — Open Workshop)*

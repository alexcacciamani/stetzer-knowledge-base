# Marketing and Branding Your Construction or Design-Build Company: A Comprehensive Guide from Builder's Edge

Erin Stetzer's approach to marketing and branding is radically different from what most builders expect. Across dozens of sessions, she consistently teaches that **your brand is not your logo — it's how you show up every single day**, and that **operational excellence IS your marketing engine**. Here's a thorough synthesis of everything the program teaches on this topic.

---

## 1. Brand Identity Is Personal, Not Corporate

Erin's most foundational teaching on branding is that in construction, your personal identity and your company brand are inseparable. This appears as early as the program kickoff and is revisited in the dedicated brand identity session near year's end.

> "Aligning your identity with your brand, because they're one in the same, it's not company and it's not person. It's one thing. And so all of that needs to align for you to really grow your business, because that is authentic."
> — **2024-03-20 — Builder's Edge Program Kickoff Call**

In the **2025-12-18 — Brand Identity and Seasons of Business** session, Erin expanded this into five pillars of brand identity:

1. **How you show up IS your brand** — "Pretend there's a camera following you around all day long."
2. **Clients are purchasing your judgment** — "When someone's actually going to hire you and trust you and put their hard-earned money into you, they're actually saying, hey, I like the way you judge how to do things."
3. **If you don't share it, your brand has no identity** — "The responsibility we have is to share who we are, share how we think."
4. **Identity-based standards drive brand** — "I am the type of person that does X, and I have no opinion about it because it's the right thing to do — that forms a brand."
5. **Deliver consistently** — "Be the person that shows up, be the partner that serves, be the person others can rely on over and over and over again. And that is the operating system."

## 2. Operations and Systems ARE Your Marketing

This is perhaps Erin's most counter-intuitive and most repeated teaching. She has never relied on traditional marketing to grow her business. Instead, exceptional operational performance generates referrals and repeat business organically.

> "I didn't know anybody in Houston. There are six million people in Houston. I didn't know anybody. Not one person. The bread and butter of all of this is in operations and that's the selling part."
> — **2025-05-08 — Open Workshop: Lien Waivers, Modifications, Scaling**

> "You don't have to sell anything. They're in the next meeting with a client that came to them and they're saying, well, wait a minute, Jennifer would be a great fit for this project. They're selling. That's because of these systems."
> — **2025-05-08**

In the **2025-06-10 — Brand Identity and Social Media Consistency** session, Erin taught that sharing your actual systems and processes — not just claiming quality — is what differentiates you:

> "We don't get the job. Well, now the architects and designers are saying, hey, let's go work with Aaron, Aaron Riddle, because you've shared this. And then it just sort of like gets bigger and bigger."

This was reinforced in the **2025-11-13 — Proposal Presentation Framework** session, where Erin explained that when she presents a proposal, the systems themselves (weekly reports, four-week calendars, budget tracking) do the selling:

> "The fees really aren't a hard justification because it's pretty clear that we're probably the most expensive person you're talking to. We do what we say we're going to do. You're going to get an exceptional home."

## 3. Social Media: Consistency Over Perfection

The **2025-06-10 — Brand Identity and Social Media Consistency** session was entirely dedicated to this topic. Erin's core rules:

### The Three Stories Per Day Rule
> "The rule is post three stories a day, no matter what, like that's how you're going to be consistent. And we post one thing a day on our Instagram."

### Face on Camera Builds Trust
> "When we have new business calls, it's so comfortable because they know me, but they literally know me and you skip through all the coffee talk, you know, you skip through all the BS and just get to the conversation."

### Imperfection IS Authenticity
> "It doesn't have to be formal, it doesn't have to be perfect. That's what I have learned. It just needs to be helpful."

Erin shared a memorable example of how an accidental mistake in a video went viral:
> "I accidentally said nails. And we all know you use screws to put sheet rock in the walls, but literally within like six hours later, there were 30,000 people that were now following us."

### Human Content Will Become More Valuable, Not Less
In the **2025-08-14 — AI Tools in Home Building** session, Erin emphasized:
> "We're all pretty lucky. Like we can still show up on Instagram. Nothing. I don't edit anything. I literally show up on a job site, just like you guys and you do your thing, you help. And there's going to be value to that. Just stick with it."

### Your People Will Find You
> "Your people will follow you. And the people that won't, you wouldn't have wanted to work with them anyway."
> — **2025-06-10**

## 4. The Feeder System: Your Real Marketing Strategy

Across multiple sessions, Erin teaches that the primary path to better clients is through **deliberate cultivation of "feeder" relationships** — architects, designers, vendors, and trades who refer high-quality clients to you.

### Build a List and Pursue It for Years
> "I make a list I'd still do this this year every year I have this list it will take me sometimes four years to get into the right architect four years I look back at my lists right so it's not a quick process but you have to know who that list is."
> — **2025-04-09 — Open Workshop: Feeders, Team Structure**

### How Erin Built Her Feeder Network from Zero
From the **2025-11-13 — Proposal Presentation Framework** session (full transcript):
> "I would hop into an engineer's office. I would hop into Ann Sachs — show me behind the scenes. That is all marketing. They get to know you. I didn't know that that was marketing. That was built out of curiosity and pure effort."

She specifically targeted landscape architects because "when people are spending a million to $5 million on planting, that's a good crowd to be in."

### Feeders Include Trades and Vendors
From the **2026-02-26 — How To Move A Prospect To A Client** session:
> "Don't ever discount trades and companies you work with and vendors. Some of the best projects we've been involved in were from our home automation company."

### The Architect Relationship Strategy
> "I identified five architects that I'm going to work with. And I identified five designers and I studied their work. I marched into their offices and said, I want to work with you."
> — **2024-03-20 — Program Kickoff**

In the **2025-05-29 — Budget vs. Actual** session, she elaborated:
> "I stalked them on Instagram. I would ask a landscape architect to introduce me to an architect. I would work my trade base heavily. I was very much studying the architects and designers work and just trying to learn from these amazing people."

## 5. The Seven Methods for Attracting Better Clients

In the **2025-11-13 — Proposal Presentation Framework** session, Erin codified her approach into seven methods:

1. **Lead with Expertise** — "It's literally leading with your expert knowledge... You're wanting to improve a situation for somebody else. That doesn't mean be the hero. That means be a guide."
2. **Show How You Think** — "Those are the breadcrumbs right in front of us. We all have breadcrumbs literally in front of us. And those of us that actually pick those breadcrumbs up win."
3. **Demonstrate Organization** — "The number one thing that I've seen people pick up on is organization. They want to feel how organized you are... because they're about to spend a whole bunch of money with you."
4. **Build Solid Partnerships** — "Work with great companies. Don't work with junkie companies. Don't accept lower standards."
5. **Hold Standards** — "Standards are an internal game... Those who respect and want to be around those standards are attracted to that company."
6. **Upgrade Your Identity** — "Hang out with the people we respect, talk to the people we admire, reach out to those who do better than us, learn."
7. **Deliver Consistency Consistently** — "Be the person that shows up, be the partner that serves, be the person others can rely on over and over and over and over again."

## 6. The Proposal as a Marketing Tool

The **2025-11-13** session included a walkthrough of Erin's actual client proposal booklet. Key elements:

- **Table of contents** — signals organization
- **Company and product overview** — who you are
- **Team introduction with photos** — people hire people
- **Communication systems** — weekly reports, four-week calendars
- **Budget management approach** — how you handle money
- **Client references AND professional references** — architects, designers who vouch for you

Erin's process: meet clients at a house under construction, evaluate fit through five or six detail-related observations, and only hand them the proposal if she senses a good fit:
> "I put this somewhere in the house where if I think they're a good fit, I'll hand it to them. If I don't think we're a great fit, I won't even hand it to them just because we're done."

## 8. The Shift: From "Who You Know" to "Who Knows You"

Erin explicitly names this paradigm shift in the **2025-11-13** session:
> "The world is changing. Like it used to be, you know, it's who you know. Now it's who knows you."

This means:
- **Visibility is non-negotiable** — even if you don't like social media
- **Content doesn't need to be polished** — raw job site videos from your iPhone are sufficient
- **Outsourcing is fine** — Erin's social media costs $3,000–$9,000/month, but she generates all content herself from her iPhone
- **Consistency matters more than production value**

## 9. Practical Marketing Tactics from the Community

Across sessions, participants and Erin shared specific tactical ideas:

### Low-Cost, High-Impact
- **Signature items**: Custom candles, branded pins, quality pens (Brittany, **2025-12-18**)
- **Premium signage**: "The bougiest sign" at every job site
- **Branded apparel**: Shirts for install crews with website
- **Community events**: Host art shows, wine walks, pop-up galleries in completed or listed spaces (Oliver, **2025-11-13**)
- **Spec homes as marketing**: Host community events in completed spec homes (**2025-09-22**)

### Relationship-Based Marketing
- **Visit showrooms and design centers** to learn AND to be known
- **Attend book signings, industry events, chef competitions** — even the free crowd section
- **Know your trades personally** — "I know our go-to tile guys' birthdays" (Bernie, **2025-11-13**)
- **Get into publications through designer relationships** rather than paying for placement

### What NOT to Do
- Don't spend heavily on branding polish before your identity has settled (**2025-12-18**)
- Be cautious with brand sponsorships — "Unless it's something I really believe in and I'm using, I would want to be the one to approach them" (**2025-11-13**)
- Don't let AI write your communications verbatim — "It sounds terrible. You know, it sounds like you're a robot" (**2025-08-14**)
- Neighborhood magazines are often "a huge waste of money" (Brittany, **2025-12-18**)

## 10. Marketing at Different Business Stages

### Years 0–5: Hustle and Learn
> "Zero to five years into this business, it is take every project, learn, learn, learn, make it happen."
> — **2025-07-24**

Marketing at this stage is pure effort: visiting showrooms, meeting trades, showing up at events, saying yes to projects to build your portfolio and knowledge base.

### Years 5–10: Refine and Position
Patterns emerge about who you serve well. Begin filtering clients, building your feeder list deliberately, and investing in consistent social media presence.

### Years 10+: Systems Sell for You
> "Most of our business, 90 percent, is repeat with architects and designers just because now we're gelled as teams."
> — **2025-05-29**

At this stage, your reputation, systems, and relationships generate work without traditional marketing.

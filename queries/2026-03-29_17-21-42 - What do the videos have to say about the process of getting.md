# Business Development: Getting New Business, Clients, and Partners

Erin Stetzer's coaching covers business development extensively across dozens of sessions. Rather than treating it as a discrete "sales" function, she frames it as an integrated system where operations, relationships, personal identity, and deliberate positioning compound over time to generate work. Here's a comprehensive synthesis.

---

## 1. The Feeder System: Building Relationships That Generate Work

The single most important business development concept in Builder's Edge is the **feeder system** — deliberately cultivating relationships with architects, designers, and other professionals who refer high-quality clients to you.

**Identify and pursue feeders intentionally:**

> "I make a list I'd still do this this year every year I have this list it will take me sometimes four years to get into the right architect four years I look back at my lists right so it's not a quick process but you have to know who that list is."
> — *2025-04-09 — Open Workshop: Feeders, Team Structure, Design-Build Strategy*

Erin teaches making an annual list of five target architects and five target designers, studying their work, and proactively approaching them — even before you're "ready":

> "I identified five architects that I'm going to work with. And I identified five designers and I studied their work. I marched into their offices and said, I want to work with you. I don't know if I'm here yet, but if there's anything, any project you want me to bid on, I'm in."
> — *2024-03-20 — Builder's Edge Program Kickoff Call*

**Use your trade network to build introductions:**

> "I stalked them on Instagram. I would ask a landscape architect to introduce me to an architect. I would work my trade base heavily."
> — *2025-05-29 — Budget vs. Actual*

> "I talked to the trim carpenter. Hey, which painters do you love working with that love working with you? And then he would give me two and I would go look at their work."
> — *2025-09-18 — Bridge to Bigger Builds*

In the **2026-02-26 session (How To Move A Prospect To A Client)**, Erin expanded this further:

> "Don't ever discount trades and companies you work with and vendors. Some of the best projects we've been involved in were from our home automation company."

She also advises looking beyond the obvious — photographers who shoot high-end homes, luxury magazine events, art auctions — anywhere target clients and design professionals gather.

**The long game:** Feeder relationships can take years to develop. By the time they mature, they become the primary pipeline:

> "Most of our business, 90 percent, is repeat with architects and designers just because now we're gelled as teams."
> — *2025-05-29 — Budget vs. Actual*

## 2. Operations and Systems ARE the Sales Engine

A recurring theme across nearly every session is that **exceptional operations generate referrals more effectively than any marketing effort.**

> "You don't have to sell anything. They're in the next meeting with a client that came to them and they're saying, well, wait a minute, Jennifer would be a great fit for this project. They're selling. That's because of these systems."
> — *2025-05-08 — Open Workshop: Lien Waivers, Modifications, Scaling*

> "I didn't know anybody in Houston. There are six million people in Houston. I didn't know anybody. Not one person. The bread and butter of all of this is in operations and that's the selling part."
> — *2025-05-08*

Erin's proposal booklet — presented in detail in the **2025-11-13 session (Proposal Presentation Framework)** — showcases systems rather than selling services:

> "The fees really aren't a hard justification because it's pretty clear that we're probably the most expensive person you're talking to. We do what we say we're going to do. You're going to get an exceptional home."

The client story drives custom home referrals:

## 3. The First Client Meeting: Serve First, Sell Never

Erin is emphatic that the first interaction with a prospect should be **entirely about service**:

> "Whenever I meet a client for the first time, whether it's a phone call or a zoom or an in-person meeting, that entire meeting is about how I can walk away and serve them. I have no, this is not about me."
> — *2024-03-20 — Kickoff Call*

The goal is to leave them with three clear pieces of actionable information. This builds trust organically and creates a natural relationship progression.

In the **2025-08-07 session (How To Move A Prospect To A Client)**, Erin formalized this into a four-step framework:

1. **Create common ground** — listen deeply for three to five needs before responding
2. **Show genuine excitement** — if the project passed your filter, let enthusiasm show
3. **Do the work** — study budgets, plans, and details so your competence is visible
4. **Add value through tangible deliverables** — show up with physical binders of bids, schedules, and process documents

> "People are tactical they're visual they want to know that you've done the work without you talking about it like they can see it."

**Active listening is paramount:**

> "What you're really listening for the entire time is the need you're waiting and waiting and waiting to listen for the need or needs."
> — *2025-08-07*

**Acknowledge client vulnerability:**

> "You have to remember they're vulnerable they've they're investing in whoever they work with they really are in an unknown space."
> — *2025-08-07*

## 4. Qualifying Prospects and Saying No

Erin teaches that **selectivity is a growth strategy**, not a luxury:

> "The answer no is a really powerful answer. And unless we take that jump... clients arrive, like when that word gets out, they find you."
> — *2025-05-22 — Open Workshop*

**Require commitment before investing time:**

> "Are you if you're 99% sure that I'm your builder? Then my next step is to start putting pricing together for you. But you need to tell me that... I need to know that because I invest a lot of time in my clients."
> — *2025-05-22*

**Meet in person and walk a completed home before pricing anything:**

> "I literally don't do any pricing unless I meet the clients in person. I make them walk through a home that we've built before I spend any time, even with a feasibility study."
> — *2025-05-22*

In the **2025-07-24 session (Clarifying Your Ideal Client)**, Erin laid out the filtering framework based on **people, time, and money**:

> "When it comes to clarifying our ideal client, it's so much more than just the person hiring you. It's the time, it's the people, it's the money."

She also teaches using **scorecards** for every project participant — architect, designer, trade, and client — to build a data-driven picture of who you work well with:

> "I score them, every designer we work with, every architect we work with, every client that we work with over and over and over again, because that's going to give you feedback. I promise you, if you do this, you speed yourself up five years."
> — *2025-07-24*

**Goal clarity drives project selection:**

> "The more that happens you'll be saying no nine times out of ten like it's nice that they're coming to you but you've got to be really clear on where you're going."
> — *2025-04-09*

## 5. Social Media and Brand Visibility

Erin treats social media — particularly Instagram — as a **pre-selling engine** that eliminates cold introductions:

> "When we have new business calls, it's so comfortable because they know me, but they literally know me and you skip through all the coffee talk, you know, you skip through all the BS and just get to the conversation."
> — *2025-06-10 — Brand Identity and Social Media Consistency*

**The three stories per day rule:**

> "The rule is post three stories a day, no matter what, like that's how you're going to be consistent. And we post one thing a day on our Instagram."
> — *2025-06-10*

**Face on camera builds trust:**

> "People feel most comfortable when they feel like they know something about you even though they've never met you."
> — *2026-02-26 — How To Move A Prospect To A Client*

**Authenticity over production quality:**

> "It doesn't have to be formal, it doesn't have to be perfect. That's what I have learned. It just needs to be helpful."

In the **2025-12-18 session (Brand Identity and Seasons of Business)**, Erin framed visibility as an obligation:

## 8. The Seven Methods Framework (Proposal Presentation)

In the **2025-11-13 session (Proposal Presentation Framework)**, Erin synthesized her approach into seven methods for attracting better clients:

1. **Serve First** — every interaction begins with how you can help
2. **Be Magnetic** — energy and genuine care draw people toward you
3. **Lead with Expertise** — be a trusted guide, not a salesperson
4. **Show How You Think** — share your perspective in everyday conversations
5. **Solid Partnerships** — work only with high-quality collaborators
6. **Standards as an Internal Game** — hold yourself to standards that attract aligned people
7. **Deliver Consistency Consistently** — be the partner everyone can count on

> "Be the person that shows up, be the partner that serves, be the person others can rely on over and over and over and over again. And that is the operating system."

She also teaches **walking prospects through an active construction site** as a qualifying tool:

> "I put this somewhere in the house where if I think they're a good fit, I'll hand it to them. If I don't think we're a great fit, I won't even hand it to them just because we're done."

## 10. Continuous Competence as a Business Development Tool

From the **2026-02-26 session**, Erin positioned ongoing learning as directly connected to winning new work:

> "Every meeting I have a follow-up with whatever I learned during that meeting every day. That's one way to do it. That speeds it up fast."

> "It's important to really analyze at least weekly what you're known for, because when you get a better understanding of what you're really good at, it becomes clearer to deliver that message for future clients."

She also teaches **mental scenario rehearsal** — playing out client conversations, objections, and project challenges in advance so you respond with calm confidence when real situations arise.

## Summary: The Core Business Development Philosophy

Across all sessions, Erin's business development framework can be distilled into a few interconnected beliefs:

- **Your operations are your marketing.** Great systems, transparency, and delivery create referrals that outperform any outbound effort.
- **Relationships compound.** Building feeders is a multi-year investment that ultimately generates 80-90% of business.
- **Selectivity attracts.** Saying no to misaligned clients positions you as premium and draws better clients organically.
- **Show up authentically and consistently.** Social media, in-person presence, and sharing how you think all pre-sell before a meeting ever happens.
- **Serve first, always.** The first conversation is never about you — it's about how you can help them make their next decision.

As Erin summarized in the kickoff call:

> "It's about putting yourself two years ahead of the game now and doing the work while you're still feeding your family."

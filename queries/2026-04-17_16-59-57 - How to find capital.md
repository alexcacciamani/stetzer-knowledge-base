# How to Find Capital for Your Building Business

Erin and the Builder's Edge community have addressed capital sourcing across multiple sessions, primarily through the **Spec Builders Roundtable** discussions. Here's a comprehensive synthesis of everything taught on this topic.

---

## 1. Private Investors: The Primary Capital Source

### The 50/50 Equity Partnership Model

The most frequently discussed structure is bringing in a silent equity partner who funds the deal while you contribute the expertise and execution.

> "I use partners on all my new builds. So I come in with no money out of pocket. Partner gets 50% of the profit. I get 50% of profit. I do charge a GC fee of 10% ish of the build price and I do get a real estate commission on the backend."
> — **2025-11-11 — Spec Builders Roundtable: Financing, Margins, and Building Strategy**

In this model, the builder earns income three ways:
- **GC fee** (~10% of build cost) paid through the construction draw
- **Real estate commission** on the back end
- **50% of remaining profit** after all costs

### Investor Return Expectations

Erin and roundtable participants were specific about what investors expect:

> "25 is the number. Below that it's tough to find money. Below 20, like, cause there's less risky investments."
> — **2025-11-11 — Spec Builders Roundtable**

The competitive range for attracting private capital is **25-40% annualized return**. Below 20%, investors can find safer alternatives.

### How to Find Private Investors

The group emphasized that finding investors is a **relationship and visibility exercise**, not a formal solicitation process:

> "I want to make it really easy for them to say no because if you want to say no I don't want you. I don't want your money. I don't want you to ask me about everything."
> — **2025-11-11 — Spec Builders Roundtable**

**Specific tactics discussed:**

- **Share your work on social media** — investors are often people who admire what you build and want exposure to real estate without the operational burden
- **Tell your network what you're doing** — keeping the ask low-pressure ensures only genuinely interested investors say yes
- **Pitch mortgage brokers** — they frequently seek real estate LP opportunities and understand the economics
- **Understand investor psychology** — many private investors are emotionally engaged with the building process itself:

> "A lot of the people that invest in me are kind of like they wish they were builders or developers and they're kind of living vicariously through me."
> — **2025-11-11 — Spec Builders Roundtable**

- **Prepare a clean presentation deck** — even a simple one signals professionalism:

> "Having something like this just — it's a good presentation and I've gotten a lot of good feedback that like, oh you look organized. It's like, oh this is all it takes for you to believe in me? Okay cool."
> — **2025-11-11 — Spec Builders Roundtable**

### Keep Investor Count Low

> "I only have one investor per project cause I don't want to be babysitting a bunch of people."
> — **2025-11-11 — Spec Builders Roundtable**

Limiting each project to a single investor simplifies communication, reduces management overhead, and avoids conflicts between multiple stakeholders.

## 2. Bank Construction Loans

### Building a Lending Relationship Network

Multiple roundtable participants stressed that **no single bank will reliably fund you indefinitely**:

> "There's three banks that I'm using right now because periodically the banks stop lending on new construction for spec. So you have to build relationships with several banks."
> — **2025-11-11 — Spec Builders Roundtable**

**Actionable advice:** Cultivate relationships with **at least three local banks** simultaneously so you always have access to capital.

### Using Land as Collateral

If you already own lots free and clear, using them as collateral for a bank construction loan is **far more capital-efficient** than giving up equity:

> "If you own the lots, why have you thought about just using them as the collateral for a bank loan? Because you'll save a lot of money. If you're talking about giving up 50% of your equity versus paying between seven and a half and eight and a half percent, those are drastically different returns."
> — **2025-11-11 — Spec Builders Roundtable**

### Paying Yourself Through the Draw

Banks generally accept project management and superintendent fees as legitimate draw items but resist paying out profit before sale:

> "The bank is totally cool with this fee, but not the 15, 20% overhead. At least in my market."
> — **2026-01-13 — Spec Build Finishes, Budget Strategy, and Deal Financing**

Structure your compensation as a **project management fee** line item in the draw rather than as profit or overhead.

## 3. Cash Flow Management as a Capital Strategy

Beyond finding external capital, Erin teaches that **internal cash flow discipline is itself a form of capital management**.

### The 10% Upfront Deposit as a Float

> "Require a 10% upfront deposit from clients and hold it through the end of the project as a float to cover expenses between draws, effectively letting the client self-fund a line of credit."
> — **2025-09-04 — Cash Flow Projection and Business Financial Planning**

### Cash Flow Projection as a Decision-Making Tool

> "You can make decisions on your next moves or your next three moves when you can capture predictability in three months, six months, nine months, 12 months."
> — **2025-09-04 — Cash Flow Projection and Business Financial Planning**

Erin emphasized building a cash flow projection spreadsheet that separates revenue by business unit and tracks expenses monthly — this visibility is what enables confident decisions about when to take on debt, when to hire, and when to pursue new projects.

### Know Your Current Position

Kelli Little, Erin's financial partner, shared a simple formula:

> "If your business were to shut down tomorrow and you collect whatever draws are outstanding and you add that to your bank account and you cut all of your checks how much cash do you have left and that's your current position."
> — **2025-11-20 — Guest Expert Kelli Little on Construction Financial Systems**

## 4. Profitability Targets That Protect Capital

### Spec Build Profit Targets

> "You want to make 30% profit. You might end up with 20, but the number to shoot for is 30."
> — **2025-12-09 — Spec Home Selling Strategies, Lot Selection, and Market Timing**

And on the developer's minimum:

> "I want to make a 20% return on costs. So the total project costs after interest and selling fees and holding costs, I want to make 20% on the money that I have invested."
> — **2025-11-11 — Spec Builders Roundtable**

### Long-Term Scaling Benchmark

> "Your net profit wants to grow 20% a year. Like to me, I would look at big picture. Like it wants to grow 20% a year."
> — **2025-09-22 — Team Structure, Incentives, Pre-Construction Pricing, and Cash Flow**

## 5. Creative and Alternative Approaches

### Land as a Partnership Vehicle

A participant in the **2026-02-10 — Spec Building, Pre-Sales, Agent Relationships** session discussed contacting owners of vacant lots via **handwritten letters** and offering to partner or place a sign, capturing buyers who want to build — a way to access deals without capital.

### Transferring Property into an LLC

> "Transfer land into an LLC and consider adding a small-percentage partner to potentially qualify for financing that would otherwise be denied on a personal-use property."
> — **2025-11-11 — Spec Builders Roundtable**

This tactic was specifically discussed for builders who are being rejected for owner-GC personal residence loans.

### Spec Home as Model Home

When a spec home sits longer than expected:

> "It has acted as a model home and I've gotten a handful of builds in the last six months just from walking people through it."
> — **2026-01-13 — Spec Build Finishes, Budget Strategy, and Deal Financing**

A stalled spec can generate custom build leads — turning a capital problem into a marketing asset.
